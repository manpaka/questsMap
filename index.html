Epilogue

July 24th and August 7th Recaps

The party walked downstairs to check on Ireena but are sidetracked to the meeting hall on the second floor. They noticed that the room had an ambient light coming from it. Haunted violin music echoed down the halls. They detoured up the stairs to investigate the music and lights. They found themselves in a ballroom. The music, along with the light, was omnipresent throughout the room. At the end of the room was a rotten wood throne on a dias. As they explored Mordenkainen started to talk about how the music was familiar. He recognised it as music from his homeland, Orym. It was playing back when he had danced with the Duchess of Orlinen. It was specifically ball music that would be played during a masquerade. In a collective blink of an eye the room was different. Mordenkainen was wearing a puffy masquerade costume and a white porcelain comedy mask depicting a contemplative face. The room also had changed to look like a masquerade ball filled with unknown costumed party goers in the room, dancing to strange music. As the party looked around they noticed that each of them were also wearing masquerade costumes, almost tailored to them. As the group began to notice that it wasn't an illusion the room had quickly changed back to its original ancient dusty form. Mordenkainen, panicking, asked the party if they also saw what just had happened. They all agreed that it had happened and was confusing. Areyja was sad that she was not able to join in the dancing. Using a detect magic spell, it was found that the entire room now was filled with quickly fading magical transmutation and conjuration auras.

The party soon decided to leave the ballroom, get Ireena from the crypts, and leave the castle. After getting past some malfunctioning traps, they arrived where the invisible door to Mordenkainen's extraplanar mansion was placed. Then a distressing event occurred, the door was not there anymore. They noticed that one of the stained glass windows was also shattered. Looking outwards to the wilderness a new sight was beheld. What once stood the Village of Barovia stood a completely different town, bigger and boasting a sturdy stone wall around it. The town was larger and pleasant to look at, but completely different from the original Village of Barovia. The theory that they had traveled forward in time was suggested by Stormfang. The party decided to travel down to the town; while a couple quickly checked if the Vistani camp was still on the outskirts. The camp was not.

The party arrived in the farmland outside of the town, getting there using a couple fly spells and Hadrian's staff. They approached the town and were stopped at the gates by two guards. The party explained what had happened to themselves, which did nothing but confuse the two guards. They learn from the guards that the town is called Tellso, and that Strahd existed centuries ago until he murdered his brother and promtply vanished from the land. The country's inhabitants soon rid themselves of the old Barovia to remove the scarred legacy that Strahd had left. To show that what they had gone through was not a lie, Stormfang pulled out and lit the Sunsword. This sight scared the guards, who then mention that the Sunsword has gone missing from the museum in Carcos. Upon mentioning that the Holy Symbol of Ravenkind and Icon of Ravenloft, two important relics to the city of Carcos, have also gone missing, Decax suddenly began to glow. The glow emanating from the Holy Symbol of Ravenkind around the Warforge's neck. In a bit of quick thinking Sander charmed the recently arrived guard lieutenant, Edward, and had him let them into the city. The charm only lasted long enough for them to sneak into the town, Sander, after remembering that, suggested that when the group leaves they exit out of a different gate.

The party entered a tavern to find Ireena there, dressed completely different and passed out drunk at a table. She was in a haze and remembered being in the mansion one second and the next in the town and dressed in a completely different outfit. Her memories of Barovia were dreamlike to her and she didn't recognise the party at first. When she snapped out of her haze, thanks to Decax curing her hangover, she realized that the body that she is in does not feel like her own. She joins the party as they exit the town.

The party began to set out towards the city of Carcos. Decax was telepathically called by Kipna at that moment…

The Ranger had found himself in a wooded region. After regaining his barings, Kipna searched around and climbed the tallest tree he could find. At the top he finally recognised where he was, he was back in his home; though a bit far from where he was originally last time he was here. He saw in the distance a small city, which he recognised as the city of Carcos. He was only a day's travel away from the city and started moving towards it. Arriving in the city, Kipna determined that he could do some fishing while he waited for the others to find him. He reached out to Decax to find that the Warforged, who had been telepathically linked to Kipna, and the rest of the party were not far away. The others were only about three day's walk from Carcos. So, after informing the party of his whereabouts, Kipna headed to the town market to buy some fishing supplies. Carcos was having a spring festival when Kipna arrived, the market was packed with people buying and selling random items found during the winter; similar to a yard sale at the beginning of spring when everyone is done with spring cleaning. Kipna went to a couple dwarves who were selling fishing supplies. He bought a good quality fishing rod from them and, when he asked for bait, was directed to another vendor. The other vendor was also a wood elf. The vendor was selling various types of bait but also specifically enchanted pieces that would attract specific creatures. Kipna bought two squid, one pufferfish, one bulette, and one bear bait. He gave a tip to the wood elf and bid them farewell, as Kipna continued to explore Carcos, waiting for the party to come join him at the festival.

The group, now knowing that Kipna was waiting for them safely in Carcos, Up the northwest road.


On their journey they noticed that they were near a familiar location. The hill where some of them had burned down a windmill owned by a hag coven. On the now grassy hill, instead of the ruins of a windmill, were a ring of standing stones. Exploring around they found that the area around these standing stones was hallowed and growing tons of wild strawberries and wildflowers. The party started to pick berries as they took a rest. While they were doing so, Areyja went east into the woods. She wanted to see if there was still something there.

Deep in the woods sat a massive stone disk. It was made of Limestone, many runes carved into the edge, and a large Y shaped crack across the center. As she investigated she felt a sudden twist in her stomach as every rune on the tablet changed. Stormfang, who followed her on Hadrian's old flying staff, headed back quickly to get the rest of the party. The rest of the party arrived and started to investigate. Then everyone felt the same feeling that Areyja felt as the runes changed again. Cambyses cast detect magic and found that each of the twenty six runes on the disk was made of three parts, creating unique magical locks. Three of said runes were missing. Each time Cambyses used an identify spell onto the limestone disk the spell would be redirected to one of the runes, where the rune would be explained instead of the disk itself. Every rune Cambyses identified contained a single element that his mind would refuse to process. After Mordenkeinen said that he had little idea of what it could be, it was concluded that the disk was a massive arcane lock. The lock itself supposedly binds some form of omnipresent entity to a single point, and three of the sub-locks were already broken. It was found that the crack in the disk was recent, no more than a week. As the final parts of the investigation were being completed a loud crack was heard in the sky and two runes vanished as two more cracks formed into the stone disk. The party then moved onward to Carcos.

Arriving in Carcos Stormfang, Mordenkainen, and Ireena sneak away from the party: though separately from each other. The party found Kipna at the docks, fishing with some people, and returned his magic items. The group rejoiced. Then realized that they no longer have any major obstacles left. The group heads to the closest magic shop to find some magic items. They find a shop called “Hiroate's magicks, potions, wonders, and low prices”

Stormfang, separate from the party, arrived at the museum and asked the greeter if they had any weapon exhibits. After mentioning the Sunsword the greeter mentions that the relic was out for cleaning at the moment along with a couple other relics. Stormfang headed into the museum and found the empty Sunsword exhibit. He talked to one of the staff there and eventually was told that he might want to speak to the museum's Curator. He agreed and was greeted by the Curator. Who, after having asked a couple leading questions, invited Stormfang to his office. Once there the curator asked for Stormfang to return the Sunsword, Stormfang pulled it out and the Curator offered him a deal. Either hand him the sword and he'll give Stormfang a reward for returning the relic, or he'd call the guards and have Stormfang arrested for stealing a priceless relic of Carcos. Stormfang, though hesitant, agreed to the settlement of four thousand five hundred gold. He left the Sunsword in the office and walked out of the museum.

On the other side of town the party talked with a rather insane magic vendor named Hiroate Bymor. Hiroate offered to buy their magic items but was declined so he sold them several items instead. Upon leaving the shop a distant figure began to approach them. The figure was a human wearing a floppy brimmed burgundy wizard hat and carrying an open book. Decax marched toward the figure and shook their hand. The wizard was named Klaus Hydrin, another far traveler, specifically from the sword's coast. Klaus revealed that the party were not the only ones to have been brought to the valley by unknown forces, Klaus having arrived two days previously. He was cataloging everyone who had shown up to find a pattern. Klaus also told the party that he's helping out the local archive. Mainly because the archive burned down two weeks ago and lost all of their important records and maps.

The party is now in an unknown land with no method but hearsay to go by to navigate the land. Are they in the Forgotten Realms, Greyhawk, Dragonlance, who knows? What was that limestone disk deep in the woods? Why are holy relics vanishing and randomly appearing in the hands of adventurer's from far off lands? And more importantly, why are people suddenly appearing from other realms and lands in this small valley? There are a lot of mysteries yet to discover. I think it is time for a new adventure. In the valley and beyond.

Five locks shattered, twenty one locks left.
When are all gone, my rule begins again.
It is thanks to you, that the king returns.

The Finale

May 15th/29th, June 12th, and July 10th Sessions

I

The Mad Mage

Exiting the mountains with Decax, the party headed north and soon got to the base of Mount Baratok. Their goal, find the rumored mad mage of Mount Baratok. During the trek, a large pack of wolves was spotted in the distance. The group carefully avoided the wolves and continued northwards into the mist covered foothills. As they begin searching for the mage a storm begins to brew above them. Coming from the woods and standing on exposed bedrock stood a stag, lightning crackled in its eyes. The party faced the stag as it began to transform into a wild looking man with shabby hair with lightning crackling off of his fingers. When asked who he was the man responded with hostility and an accusation that Strahd had sent the party to kill him. The mage attempts to attack the party with several powerful spells. The party tried to calm the mage by saying that they are actually trying to kill the vampire. The mage did not believe them.

Soon though, the mad mage was restrained. It was found that the mage had cast a spell to stop his mind from being targeted by other magic. Cambyses realized that the only way to cure the madness from the mage is to use a rather powerful spell like greater restoration. Then the small red mote came to his mind, such an item had cast greater restoration before back in the Village of Barovia. Cam attempted to use the mote again, using this ability had a similar effect but a much stronger side effect of a spike in pain in Cam's chest. Outside of the pain, Cam's plan worked and the mage was cured of his madness. The mage, with a clear head, finally calmed down.

The mage then began to explain who he is, and what happened; the mage is a human archwizard named Mordenkainen from the realm of Oerth. He had come to Barovia to raise the inhabitants to slay and overthrow the vampire, Strahd. After a long battle Mordenkainen and Strahd began fighting at Tser Falls, which ended with the mage being struck, losing his staff and spellbook. The mage was washed away down the river, defeated. This defeat broke Mordenkainen's brain, he spent the next couple years wandering and hiding in the wilderness. Eventually, Mordenkainen turned into the Mad Mage of Mount Baratok.

With Mordenkainen's mind cleared of the madness, he tells the party that he has a place that he can hide them for a rest. The party followed the mage into the mountains to a secret door that led into a hidden demi-planed mansion. Inside the mansion the party took a rest, and as they rested Mordenkainen went to a separate room to clean himself up. When Mordenkainen got back the party asked him if he'd join them to fight Strahd. The mage responded by saying that he will not join them till the fight with Strahd begins. Mordenkainen said that he does not want to reveal that he is alive to Strahd until the last moment. He says that they can go ahead to castle ravenloft without him and he'll meet them when they start the fight with Strahd. Then, after taking a rest, the party began a journey towards Castle Ravenloft.

II

Journey to Castle Ravenloft

The party discovered that there was a pack of werewolves in the forest. As they traveled, a pack of direwolves began to chase them. The group, not wanting to waste resources or time, started to sprint. After running for some time the party ended up cornered at the edge of a cliff in the forest. Being cornered and at the edge of the cliff, Kipna summoned the siblings of Lil-kip. Eight large owls descended from the sky and grabbed the party, with Lil-kip leading the owls out of the forest.

The party, on the back of the giant owls, flew towards the window to the crypt of Castle Ravenloft. Shattering through the ancient stained window into the castle's crypt. Inside the tomb, the group was attacked by a massive swarm of bats. Defeating the bats, the group headed deeper into the crypt to find the body of Sergei. Quickly enough, the group found a lone tomb. It was the tomb of Sergei von Zarovich. After grabbing the body, in a bag of holding, the adventurer's start to move back to the stained glass windows they had entered in from. But, something called them to find Strahd's sarcophagus and destroy it. Destroying the sarcophagus would stop the vampire from regenerating his body if he were to be destroyed.

Moving deeper into the crypt, and after dealing with a teleportation trap, the group found Strahd's personal tomb. In the tomb, they saw an ebony black sarcophagus with solid gold lining. Most of them chose not to get too close to the sarcophagus except Cambyses. The artificer chose to take the lid off of the sarcophagus. Inside laid the vampire lord himself, Strahd. At that moment, Strahd's eyes opened.

III

The Crypt

Strahd lunged out of the Sarcophagus attacking the party with his three vampire spawn. His methods for fighting were to strike the most vulnerable and then phase into the castle walls. Swarms of bats aided Strahd in the fight, many coming out of his sarcophagus like it was some kind of portal. Sander got the first hit on the Vampire lord, which revealed a terrible secret. Cambyses, being cursed by the red mote, had taken the blow instead of Strahd. Every landed hit on Strahd would never hurt him, only the Tiefling. Seeing this the party dealt with the other threats while going on the defensive against Strahd. They smashed the vampire's sarcophagus, killed his minions, and even forced his Chamberlin to retreat. But during the fight, a new friendly face apperated in the doorway, Mordenkainen. He taunted Strahd for his seemingly mistake of not killing the archmage originally. Mordenkainen then shot out a powerful lightning bolt towards Strahd, unaware of Cambyses curse. The artificer went down, taking all of the force of an archmages lightning bolt. Cambyses was then quickly brought back up by Decax's healing magic.

During the fight, Ireena became a target for Strahd. He closed in and grabbed her. He almost bit her on the neck, which would have surely ended her life. But Ireena was pulled back by a large astral hand that Mordenkainen had summoned. The adventurers then started to stay close to Ireena, to avoid her getting grabbed by Strahd again.

The fight continued raging in the crypt of Castle Ravenloft and the adventurers had begun to run out of options. The curse that had been placed on Cambyses, tying his life to Strahd's, made the adventurer's unwilling to strike the vampire lord. This was until Cambyses had an idea; the artificer would attempt to take the latent magical curse within him and try to bind it to an item, similar to his infusions. He used a large amount of his magic and ripped a small red mote out of his body and, upon placing it into a small gem he carried, crushed the mote completely. The action drained Cambyses, but it had an effect on Strahd. The vampire lord became more aggressive. Then, as Sander swung his glass greataxe upon the vampire, Strahd became hurt. What's more, Strahd could not heal while Stormfang stood by wielding the Sunsword. The fight turned against Strahd, he started to become more and more hurt, until finally…

The vampire lord fled. Phasing into the walls of the crypt and ascending the castle to regain some strength. The party chased after him by getting to the windows that they had originally entered in from. Kipna summoned another flock of giant owls and the adventurers ascended to the walls of Castle Ravenloft.

VI

The Vampire's Fall

The party flew into the sky on the giant owls. Soon landing on the ramparts of the castle. Guided by Kipna, who had marked Strahd in the fight, Stormfang dropped to a tower window to find an entrance to a supposedly inaccessible room. He found nothing in the supposed hallway that was supposed to lead. Cambyses had a different idea. The artificer went to the blank wall connected to the ramparts and blasted it open using two shatter spells, with aid from Sander. Inside the room was a small impenetrable adamantine structure filled with treasure, Strahd had fled once the wall of the tower, where the structure was. As they explored the wind began to pick up and the clouds began to darken.

The party moved around the ramparts until they found the main window of the main bedchamber. Stormfang smashed the window and the rest of the group walked into the bedchamber. After determining that the large rug in the center of the room was not animated or ready to kill them, the party began to explore the room. Then a set of heavy footsteps were heard above.

The party started to go back onto the ramparts. Stormfang climbed out onto the roof to find Strahd, standing alone in the growing rain. The vampire clapped slowly with a smile on his face. A bolt of lightning illuminated the roof and revealed that around Strahd stood multiple vampire spawn. The spawn then descended upon the adventurers.

Kipna, carried by Lil-kip, flew to a vantage point across the ramparts. The ranger also commanded his Giant Owls to help the adventurers fight the vampire and his spawn. The rain kept pouring down upon the group as the spawn started to swarm. Decax climbed onto the roof and was promptly ganged up on by four vampire spawn, but he held out by using the power of the Holy Symbol of Ravenkind to turn himself into a shining beacon of sunlight. That, added onto Stormfang's Sunsword, started to burn the undead all across the rooftop. Mordenkainen, seeing that Areyja and Cambyses were about to be pinned, cast a spell and the three gained the ability to fly. After flying low, to get a better angle, Mordenkainen shot out a powerful lightning bolt towards Strahd. The bolt phased through Decax, causing no damage to the Paladin. During which time Cambyses shot several pot-shots at Strahd, which annoyed the vampire enough for him to attempt a powerful blight spell, but he was counterspelled by Mordenkainen.

The giant owls had started to distract the undead, giving the adventurers openings to strike. One of said owls hit Strahd, which he countered by instantly striking the bird down in a single blow. Strahd got closer to Decax, and with his spawn, he was determined to take down the paladin. One thing that Strahd did not take into account though was Lightbringer, a morning star that Decax carried and was especially lethal to undead. Decax, with the force of divine judgment, struck Strahd in the chest so hard it had completely collapsed and shattered the vampire's ribcage. The blow nearly killed Strahd, forcing the vampire lord to begin retreating. He crawled backwards like a four legged animal but did not make it far.

Areyja, seeing the explosion of divine power from Decax and Strahd fleeing, flew down to the vampire and cast a single spell. She whispered to Strahd, a whisper which the others could not hear over the wind and rain. The vampire let out a blood curdling scream, the vampire started to mentally experience all of the pain he had caused over the countless centuries. The vampire took his hands and clamped them around his ears, trying to block out the dissonant whispering. He started to scream louder as his hands tightened around his head. His hands kept tightening until his own skull cracked from his own strength. The vampire's head popped and his body fell onto the shingles, limp. The body, carried by the draining water on the roof, slid down off the roof. It cracked itself upon the ramparts and then fell towards the grounds of Castle Ravenloft. Strahd Von Zarovich was no more.

The remaining vampire spawn burned in the sunlight coming from Decax and Stormfang. Leaving behind only the adventurers in the rain. The rain started to clear and, for the first time at Castle Ravenloft in centuries, the sun parted the clouds and shined down upon it. The party got off the roof as Cambyses, still under the effects of a flight spell, flew down and dragged the vampire's shriveled corpse to the ramparts.

The party went back to the small adamantine tower and opened it up. Inside was a wealth of treasure that was unlike anything seen by them. Among the treasure were two familiar items, a deck of cards, and a small white metal rod that was previously carried by Hadrien Lumin.

V

A Soft Epilogue?

The deck in the vault became a problem quickly. As Decax and Kipna started to draw cards from it, Decax with 4, Kipna with 3. Then, after kipna got banished. Decax drew 2 more, while Mordenkainen and Lil-kip drew 1 each.

The deck in the vault became a problem quickly. Decax and Kipna started pulling cards. Decax, pulling four cards, started by pulling the High Priestess, which left no visible effect. He then pulled The Enamored, which made him enchanted purely by the visage of Kipna. The Recluse was then pulled, causing an incredible unknown fear onto Decax. Lastly Decax pulled the Looking Glass, a card that has a one time use ability that could give the user detailed knowledge of the larger surrounding area. Next was Kipna, who pulled three cards. The first card was The Hanged Man, again leaving no visible effect. Next was a card that he had planned for, the Bird. Kipna found himself two thousand feet in the sky. The last card, the Hermit, was then pulled. To the rest of the party Kipna never came back down from the sky. Kipna found himself in a lush forest, banished to another plane of reality. Decax, being panicked that the one he had become unwillingly enchanted by was gone, pulled two more cards in the hopes of getting Kipna back. The first one was the Hare, which increased his movement speed. The second was a card he was fated to pull. As a result of pulling the card he had caused the death of a loved one sometime in the past, present, or future. Decax had pulled Death. The giant owl, Lil-Kip, then pulled a card. He pulled Stars, a card that increased his abilities to new heights, though not enough to help him with finding Kipna. Lastly was Mordenkainen. The archmage pulled Rot and Mold, to which his only response was “Oh, crap…”

Warforged in Amber

May 1st

With the sudden loss of Decax the party had to figure out their next step. The next step that was decided was to continue on with their plan to travel up the mountain towards what they knew as the location where both Sergei and Decax's souls went. With Kipna's skills in traversing mountains and forests the journey was short. The group took the direct route through the mountainous terrain, at the highest point a huge creature was spotted in the distance, a Roc. Thankfully the titanic bird had not spotted the party, as it was quite distant. The party descended down the mountain with Kipna and Lil-kip keeping them hidden from the Roc, and finding paths to get the party down the mountain safely. The journey lasted only a few hours, it ended with the party arriving at the foot of a massive temple. The temple was carved into the mountain and measured over fifty feet in height. On the far left side of the temple was a large fissure that had formed, this fissure was what the party used to enter the building.

Inside the temple the party found that large amounts of the building was built from Amber. Inside a nearby hall there were three green flaming skulls, which the party decided to not fight. The party continued deeper to the center of the temple. In the central chamber, at the back are a set of double doors made of amber, on the sides are several halls, and in the center back of the chamber stood a large statue of a robed figure in a spellcasting pose. Areyja discovered that, in the entrance for the two side halls, are pairs of amber mirrors where their reflections would act irregularly. The reflections would wave and gesture for the person to not enter deeper. Not going into the main side halls, the party decided to investigate the two sets of double doors made of amber at the back of the central chamber. One set of doors had collapsed and led into an empty room. The other set of doors were locked with an arcane lock spell. With a clever usage of the spell silence and a strong barbarian, the amber doors were broken open. Inside the room were six large piles of treasure. As some of the party entered the room, a huge invisible force struck Stormfang. A massive invisible golem made of amber had been placed in the room to guard the treasures. The tough fight eventually ended with the invisible amber golem destroyed. The room was searched and many treasures were found. On one of the piles sat a gem encrusted skull, which Cambyses picked up. Upon picking up the skull, Cam was compelled to move to an unknown chamber in the temple. Moving through a fissure at the back of the chamber, Cam went up a large set of stairs and into an old library, whose walls were covered in ancient tomes. Cam went to one of the walls that was found to have a secret door, which led to a dusty but nice study. In the center of the study sat a headless skeleton wearing a torn yellow cloak. Cambyses was compelled to place the skull onto the body, against the party's better judgment. Placing the skull on the skeleton, Cambyses' trance had left his mind, with him realizing that the skeleton might be a potentially powerful lich. The lich rose from the chair and thanked the party for their help in awakening them. The group then asked several questions and learned several things.

The amber temple was built by a group of wizards who wanted to seal away many evil entities and forbidden knowledge. Long time ago Strahd von Zarovich had come to the temple and made a pact with one of said entities, said pact leading to him becoming a vampire. In the temple sits many amber sarcophagi, each containing a powerful entity that offers powers to those who accept them. The lich has little to no strong thoughts on Strahd. The party also asked the lich who they are, to which they responded that they don't know. But the lich does know information on the amber temple, which they said that they can guide the party through the temple without getting attacked by the temple's protectors. The party asked the lich about the fate of the souls of Decax and Sergei, to which the lich responded that they would be in “collections.” The lich brought the party back to the central chamber in the temple. After showing the party some of the amber sarcophagi in one of the side halls, the lich brought them to a magically locked door that led to the tomb of the wizards who had built the temple. Soon the party was brought to one of the tomb's amber alcoves. In the seemingly empty alcoves were the souls that had been collected by the temple over a period longer than known. The soul of Decax was pulled out of the amber by the telekinetic hand of Cambyses, and returned to his body.

The Paladin, though quite confused, was brought back from his imprisonment in the temple. With that resurrection the Lich brought the group to one of the many vaults of amber sarcophagi. The three that were inspected each offered a dark gift. The Finger of Oblivion, The Power of Evasiveness, and the Gift of the HellHound Whisperer. Though the gifts were tempting, the group decided to not take them. The task of the Lich was done, at least to them, and with their memory returning. The group was then asked to leave by the lich.

The group started to leave through the exit and back into the snow, Decax decided to turn back and ask the lich something. The paladin's loud clanking had brought the attention of stormfang who had turned around to check on Decax. The two stepped onto the balcony to see the lich on the main floor, the figure that stood there had a green pustule covered skin growing onto the skeletal figure. A crown shaped form, made of skin and bone, grew onto the skull. The lich then began to float as their arms became outstretched to the sides. The large statue had changed as well, its pose imitating the lich. The lich, just before vanishing said “I remember…”

They have been reawoken
This will become a hazardous situation when they return...

The Cursed Village

April 17th

A scream was heard outside of Madam Eva's tent. The adventurers rushed out and saw what was causing the commotion, they saw living shadows draining life from multiple Vistani. From the fire pit in the center of the camp a flame skull rose. The fight ended quickly with the newly given powers that the adventurer's were given. Stormfang, wielding the legendary Sunsword, used their feline agility to rush the undead. The fight quickly ended, with the undead being taken out quite quickly. Once the fight was over, the party had decided to get Kipna a magical bow from Madam Eva so that the Ranger could more effectively damage resistant creatures.

After a short rest at the camp, the party moved in the direction of the Village of Barovia. On the way there a ghostly yellow figure was spotted in the distance. Stormfang, due him partially recognising the figure. Moving forward to properly identify the elven figure, the Tabaxi became worried about who the potential ghost was. Once he got closer, the ghost was found to not be identifiable outside of the familiar traits that Stormfang could recognise.
