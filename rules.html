Guidelines and Rules

Note, these guidelines can and will be updated when and if needed. If you have an issue with a rule or need extra clarification just message me [Isaac] and I'll clarify it with you and update the page.

What are the West Marches?

  • A West Marches campaign is a sandbox game. All adventures are opt in, most sessions don’t connect to each other, and most plot hooks are created by the players through Missions.
  • There is no regular schedule. Every session is scheduled by the players.
  • Each session has different party members from the larger group. There is normally a limit on how many can play based on the session and DM.

Rules and Guidelines

Here are some general guidelines to the Confederation Marches.

  • Follow the Discord community guidelines. [Listed on the Discord server]
  • Adventures do not happen in the main town. This is to avoid logistical problems.
  • When a session ends, the party returns to the main town and rejoins the larger group.
  • Players cannot be in the exact same party multiple sessions in a row.
  • Players can have up to two characters if they want.
    • But you can only have 1 character in an adventure at a time.
  • Leveling up is done through group xp. Completing adventures gives group xp equal to adventure level. All characters are always the same level.
  • At the end of an adventure your character always receives a point of inspiration.

Missions

What are Missions?

  • Missions are session plot hooks made by players.
  • The setting up a mission goes like this:
    1. Starts with a player character wanting to do something outside of the home base.
    2. Player sends some basic information to me, and we set up a mission posting.
    3. The player character forms a party with others along with getting a DM and scheduled session.
    4. The DM takes the mission and sets up the session.
    5. The session starts with the party leaving homebase and doing the mission.
    6. The session ends with the party heading back to the homebase.
    7. Players write up recaps and the party disbands.

The rules and guidelines for missions.

  • The DM is the one to choose the level of the missions.
  • Each player can only have 1 active mission posting at a time.
  • The level of the mission is based on the rewards, party size, and monsters encountered on the mission.
  • Mission should not be too grand in scope (ex. Villain is going to blow up the town). This is normally up to DM discretion.
When a mission’s scope is too big it’ll be split up into several missions that are connected to each other. For Example:
  • A character wants to find their missing brother and posts a quest for it.
  • The DM then decides that it is too early (They are level 4 or something) and the character probably should not find their brother in this quest. They then make the quest reward a clue towards the brother’s potential location instead.
  • Then the player posts another new quest with the added detail that they know where to go this time.
  • The session ends with the party learning that the brother was murdered and they head back to town.
  • The character then posts another quest that is about searching for the murderer.
  • And this “adventure” can go on for a while. But, it is good for DM’s to know when to stop. Normally this is up to discretion and level of the player character.

Quests

What are quests?

  • Quests are plot hooks and quests made by DMs.
  • Quests sometimes have larger scale world events. (Though still up to my discretion)
  • Quests can also be adventures that are started by guilds or NPCs specifically.
  • To join the quests, (for now) message me and I'll add you to the game.

Advice for DMs

  • You are writing a one session adventure. It’s alright if adventures need to be extended, but do not plan for them to be longer beforehand. Ideally a narrative break in between sessions is added so players can switch around.
    • If you run overtime and a second session is necessary, schedule a part 2.
    • While characters are between session parts 1 & 2 they should not join other adventures. This is to avoid continuity problems.
  • Adventures need to be self contained.
    • A little bit of railroading is not only okay, but expected.
  • Especially with Missions suggested by players, keep to the plot! Players should make measurable progress toward the stated goal.
  • The rewards for adventures (if it’s not a hunt for specific items) are determined by the random treasure tables based on adventure level.
  • Do not benefit your own characters while dming (barring things like group xp)
  • Do not have your own characters join a session you are dming.

Recaps

What are Recaps?

When a session finishes one or more players can write up a session recap for the community to read. When a recap is posted the player character is given a reward of gold or items based on the session and level.

  • Only one recap per player, per session.
  • If a recap is written in character, the player gets a better reward.
